Pain or Symptom assessment

O - Onset - "When did it start"
P - Provocation/palliation - "What started it and does anything make worse or better"
Q - Quality - "Describe the pain"
R - Region/Radiation/Referal - "Where is the pain located, point with one finger, does it move
S - Severity - "On scale of 0 to 10 with 0 being no pain to 10 worst pain imaginable how bad is it now"
T - Time - "How long have you had the pain"

C - Circulation (Cap Refil <2sec)
S - Sensation - "Look away what finger am I touching"
M - Motor / Motion "Wiggle fingers/toes"

CVA/Stroke Assessment

F - Face - Ask Patient to Smile
A - Arms - Ask Patient to hold arms outstretched
S - Speach - Ask Patient to repeat a sentance
T - Time - To react
Update ambulance control if a patient is FAST positive

D - Deformities: Is there an abnormality in the shape of a body part or organ compared to the normal shape?
C- Contusions: Do you notice bruising? In extreme cases, this can cause blood to seep, hemorrhage or extravasate into surrounding tissues.
A - Abrasions: How severe is the abrasion? Your treatment will vary depending on the severity, but be sure to always clean and remove any debris.
P - Punctures/penetrations: Penetrating trauma suggests an object didn't pass through, while perforating trauma is associated with an entrance and exit wound.
B - Burns: Treatment depends on the severity of the burn (rule of nines).
T - Tenderness: This shouldn't be confused with pain. Tenderness is triggered by discomfort when an affected area is touched.
L - Lacerations: Treatment depends on the type, cause and depth of the wound. Minor wounds heal on their own, while puncture wounds are more prone to infection.
S - Swelling

Treatments

P - Pressure Ask the casualty to apply firm direct pressure over the wound until dressing applied
E - Elevation - If the injury is to a limb, elevate above the level of the heart
E - Expose & examine - Quickly find and examine the wound(s) for any embedded objects
P - Position - Ask the victim to sit on the floor if possible
S - Shock - Treat patient for shock
